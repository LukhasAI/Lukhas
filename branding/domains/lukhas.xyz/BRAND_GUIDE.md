## Audience Profile

### Primary Audiences
- **Researchers**: AI and consciousness researchers
- **Early Adopters**: Bleeding-edge technology enthusiasts
- **LUKHAS Engineers**: Internal testing and demos
- **Hardcore AI Enthusiasts**: Those wanting to explore experimental features
- **Academic Partners**: Collaborating researchers

### Audience Mindset
- Excited by experimental, unpolished technology
- Understands and accepts instability
- Values access to cutting-edge research
- Wants to provide feedback on prototypes
- Seeks to be part of innovation process

## Visual Design & Style

### Color Palette
**Domain Accent**: **Experimental Purple/Shifting** `#A855F7` - Changing, quantum, playful

**Supporting**:
- **Dynamic Palette**: Colors shift based on experiments
- **Gradient Chaos**: Multi-color gradients (not constrained)
- **Glitch Effects**: Intentional visual experimentation
- **Neon Accents**: Bright, attention-grabbing

### Visual Motifs
- **Glimmering Nodes**: Ever-changing particle systems
- **Shifting Backgrounds**: Dynamic, not static
- **Experimental Typography**: Unusual fonts, sizes
- **Chaotic Grids**: Breaking conventional layouts
- **Warning Aesthetics**: "Under construction" vibe (intentional)

**Design Philosophy**:
- Break the rules of other domains
- Improvise, don't constrain
- Visual experimentation encouraged
- BUT: Still functional and accessible

## Experiment Structure

Each experiment page includes:
- **Codename**: Fun project codename
- **Status**: Alpha, Beta, Proof-of-Concept, Deprecated
- **What It Is**: Brief explanation
- **Why We're Testing**: Research question or use case
- **Try It**: Interactive demo or sandbox
- **Known Issues**: What doesn't work
- **Feedback**: Link to discussion or form
- **Related Research**: Papers or concepts

---

## User Experience Principles

- **Embrace Chaos**: Design doesn't have to be perfect
- **Clear Warnings**: Always mark experimental status
- **Easy Feedback**: One-click bug reports
- **Low Stakes**: Users know data may be lost
- **Discoverable**: Easy to find and explore experiments

---

## Content Quality Standards

- **Honesty**: Be upfront about limitations
- **Science**: Explain research questions
- **Playfulness**: Use humor, fun codenames
- **Rigor**: Still technically accurate (not sloppy)
- **Creativity**: Try unusual approaches

## Quick Reference

### Do's ✓

- Be transparent about experimental nature
- Have fun with naming and design
- Iterate rapidly based on feedback
- Break conventional design rules (carefully)
- Invite community participation
- Explain research context

### Don'ts ✗

- Don't pretend experiments are stable
- Don't store critical user data here
- Don't skip accessibility entirely
- Don't ignore user feedback
- Don't make experiments impossible to use
- Don't forget to document learnings
