## Overview

LUKHAS AI is committed to data protection and privacy. We comply with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and implement privacy-by-design principles in our consciousness-inspired AI platform.

This guide explains how LUKHAS ensures GDPR compliance and how you can exercise your data protection rights.

---

## 1. HOW LUKHAS PROCESSES YOUR DATA

### 1.1 What We Collect

**As a Platform User**:
- Account information (name, email)
- Authentication credentials (password hash, MFA)
- Usage data (reasoning traces, memory folds)
- Technical data (IP address, device info)

**As an End User** (via customer applications):
- Query content
- Reasoning traces
- Memory folds (if enabled)
- Usage metrics (pseudonymized)

### 1.2 Why We Collect It

- **Service Provision**: To operate the LUKHAS AI platform
- **Reasoning**: To generate consciousness-inspired AI responses
- **Memory**: To preserve context across sessions (with your consent)
- **Improvement**: To enhance our services (anonymized analytics)
- **Security**: To protect against fraud and abuse

### 1.3 Legal Basis

| Purpose | Legal Basis |
|---------|-------------|
| Platform operation | Contract (GDPR Art 6(1)(b)) |
| Memory Folds | Consent (GDPR Art 6(1)(a)) |
| Security | Legitimate Interest (GDPR Art 6(1)(f)) |
| Legal compliance | Legal Obligation (GDPR Art 6(1)(c)) |

## 2. YOUR GDPR RIGHTS

### 2.1 Right of Access (Article 15)

You can request:
- Confirmation of processing
- Copy of your personal data
- Information about processing purposes and recipients

**How to Exercise**: Email privacy@lukhas.ai or use your account dashboard

**Response Time**: Within **30 days**

### 2.2 Right to Rectification (Article 16)

You can request correction of inaccurate personal data.

**How to Exercise**: Update via account settings or email privacy@lukhas.ai

**Response Time**: Without undue delay

### 2.3 Right to Erasure / "Right to be Forgotten" (Article 17)

You can request deletion of your personal data when:
- No longer necessary for purposes collected
- You withdraw consent
- You object to processing
- Data was unlawfully processed

**How to Exercise**: Email privacy@lukhas.ai with "Data Deletion Request"

**Response Time**: Within **30 days**

**Note**: Some data must be retained for legal obligations (e.g., financial records for 7 years)

### 2.4 Right to Restriction (Article 18)

You can request restriction of processing when:
- You contest accuracy of data
- Processing is unlawful but you don't want deletion
- We no longer need data but you need it for legal claims
- You object to processing pending verification

**How to Exercise**: Email privacy@lukhas.ai

### 2.5 Right to Data Portability (Article 20)

You can request your personal data in structured, machine-readable format.

**How to Exercise**: Use account dashboard export or email privacy@lukhas.ai

**Format**: JSON or CSV

**Response Time**: Within **30 days**

### 2.6 Right to Object (Article 21)

You can object to processing based on legitimate interests or direct marketing.

**How to Exercise**:
- Marketing: Unsubscribe link in emails
- Other processing: Email privacy@lukhas.ai

**Response Time**: Immediate (marketing), 30 days (other)

### 2.7 Automated Decision-Making (Article 22)

LUKHAS AI provides reasoning assistance but does not make fully automated decisions with legal/significant effects without human oversight in customer applications.

If you believe you're subject to automated decision-making:
**Contact**: privacy@lukhas.ai

## 4. DATA RETENTION

### 4.1 Retention Periods

| Data Type | Retention Period | Reason |
|-----------|------------------|--------|
| Account data | Account lifetime + 30 days | Service provision |
| Reasoning traces | 90 days (default, configurable) | Service provision |
| Memory Folds | User-configurable with decay | User choice |
| Financial records | 7 years | Legal requirement |
| Marketing data | Until consent withdrawn | Consent-based |

### 4.2 Automatic Deletion

- Reasoning traces: Automatically deleted after retention period
- Memory Folds: Temporal decay (Ebbinghaus curve)
- Inactive accounts: After 2 years inactivity (with notice)
